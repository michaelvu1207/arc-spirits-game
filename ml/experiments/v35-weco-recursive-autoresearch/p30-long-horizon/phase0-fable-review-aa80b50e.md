There is no evidence of a 54-endpoint synthetic manifest, no label shuffling, no execution of the production analyzer through its isolation, empty-output, signing, or consumed-token paths. The contrast with the fault-injection report (input-04), which enumerates all eleven required cases one-for-one, makes the gap clear — this is not a reporting-granularity difference. Additionally, the rehearsal run's `stderrSha256` (`4f841141…`) is not the empty-input hash, so its stderr was nonempty, while the production analyzer contract requires empty stdout/stderr; the "empty-output path" the plan demands was therefore not demonstrated. Because the scheduler gate for generation-one authorization explicitly depends on this preflight, the gate is unverifiable on this evidence and the launch cannot proceed.
